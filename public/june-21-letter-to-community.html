Mission

Hi everyone!

We are entering the post-COVID world and we, as CoronaWhy, still exist. 

The world has won the fight with coronavirus psychologically but not physically, people are still dying and recent spikes are a great example of why the society has failed to address this pandemic.

I am truly fascinated by what we've created together: back in April my biggest fear was "what if all these amazing people disappear when COVID is gone" and guess what, it's not happening, we are even more active and pushing the boundaries of impossible every day! The most intelligent and the most supportive people I've ever met, and I've never met any of you in real life. And I want to, really want to have us all connect in real life at some point.

Because the work we are doing is absolutely extraordinary and recent major updates are the best proof.

Is this utopian? Is this a dream?

It's happening, we are everywhere. and open science is not just a utopian dream but an ambitious dream to pursue.

That's why I believe that we will exist tomorrow, weeks from now, and years from now. 

And maybe, someday, researchers won't have to fight for funding to cure cancer, medical journals won't have to fight for survival and make a business out of publishing papers, conferences won't be judged by the prestige of citations, young scientists won't have to struggle to survive financially and more importantly: the age, race, sex, the difference in opinions simply won't matter anymore.


What will matter is impact, transparent collaboration with a single objective function to benefit humanity, with reproducible science at its core, with companies funding collaborative research and medical journals, with governments supporting scientists by all means possible and with endless curiosity to push the boundaries of our advancements as a civilization.

‍

All these amazing things are happening not because of the money (there is none involved) but because of us being bound to our Values (what we believe in), Principles (how we deal with reality) and our Missions (our short and long term goals).

We have shown the world it's not true, we've shown the power of the individual, and the power of a community of like-minded people.

Are we an organization? Should we be one?

This week, we had a very very long call about establishing an entity, primarily because:

- We don't want to ruin the volunteer spirit of our community

- We want to stay open-source but we also want to make sure we are sustainable and people stay with us for the good and have some form of benefits of doing so, monetary or health insurance or any other important basic securities/needs

- We need funding of some sorts because currently Artur is funding all the expenses using his own personal cards and that accumulates, $5000 credits from Google are ending, free slack plan is also ending and will cost us $2500 monthly

- We need to protect members of our community from any attempts to scrutinize them, be it malicious or accidents like accidentally releasing sensitive information of 3rd parties or individuals to the open world, GDPR  and other compliances are mandatory for the nature of the work we are doing

- Our partners/sponsors that are already doing pro-bono work also need to make sure they are doing it right and have some relationship with us as an organization, like SonguePR which is a PR firm helping us with communications

- We need legal help for the above (funding, protection, partnerships) and need a legal entity for that
